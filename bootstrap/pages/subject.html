    • Kindergarden

    1. Counting:
      • Learning to count numbers from 1 to 10 or higher.
      • Counting everyday objects, like toys, fingers, or steps.
    2. Number Recognition:
      • Identifying and recognizing numbers visually.
      • Associating numbers with the quantity they represent.
    3. Basic Operations:
      • Introduction to addition and subtraction using manipulatives like counting blocks or fingers.
      • Simple word problems involving basic arithmetic.
    4. Shapes:
      • Identifying and naming basic shapes such as circles, squares, triangles, and rectangles.
      • Recognizing shapes in everyday objects.
    5. Patterns:
      • Recognizing and creating simple patterns using colors, shapes, or objects.
      • Completing given patterns.
    6. Measurement:
      • Understanding concepts of size, length, and weight.
      • Using comparative words like big/small, long/short.
    7. Spatial Awareness:
      • Understanding positional words such as up, down, under, over, beside.
      • Developing an awareness of space and location.
    8. Sorting and Classifying:
      • Sorting objects based on attributes such as color, shape, or size.
      • Classifying objects into groups.
    9. Time Concepts:
      • Introduction to basic concepts of time, such as morning, afternoon, and night.
      • Sequencing daily activities.
    10. Money:
      • Introduction to the concept of money and basic coins (penny, nickel, dime).
      • Understanding the value of coins.
    11. Data Analysis:
      • Collecting and organizing data in simple charts or graphs.
      • Drawing conclusions based on basic data sets.

    Grade 8 :Algebra

    1. Linear Equations:
      • Understanding and solving linear equations.
      • Graphing linear equations on the coordinate plane.
    2. Quadratic Equations:
      • Solving quadratic equations using factoring, completing the square, and the quadratic formula.
      • Graphing quadratic functions.
    3. Systems of Equations:
      • Solving systems of linear equations algebraically and graphically.
      • Understanding the concept of consistent and inconsistent systems.
    4. Exponents and Radicals:
      • Understanding and simplifying expressions with exponents.
      • Solving problems involving radicals and exponents.
    5. Polynomials:
      • Operations with polynomials (addition, subtraction, multiplication).
      • Factoring polynomials.
    6. Functions:
      • Defining, evaluating, and graphing functions.
      • Understanding the concepts of domain and range.
    7. Inequalities:
      • Solving and graphing linear inequalities.
      • Understanding compound inequalities.
    8. Rational Expressions:
      • Simplifying and performing operations with rational expressions.
      • Solving equations involving rational expressions.
    9. Coordinate Geometry:
      • Understanding the relationship between algebraic equations and geometric shapes on the coordinate plane.
      • Graphing linear and quadratic equations.
    10. Word Problems:
      • Solving real-world problems using algebraic expressions and equations.
      • Translating verbal expressions into algebraic expressions.
